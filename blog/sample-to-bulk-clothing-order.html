Sample approval to bulk production

How to move from approved clothing sample to bulk production without losing quality.

A good sample is only the beginning. Before a Chinese clothing factory cuts bulk fabric, buyers need a locked standard for fit, fabric, trims, labels, packing, QC, and delivery. This guide explains the checkpoints that protect your order.

Ask about bulk productionReview QC processFirst order guide
Short answer

To make bulk production match the sample, approve a golden sample or PPS, lock the bulk fabric and trims, confirm measurements and tolerance, document label and packing rules, monitor production, and inspect goods before final balance.

Why bulk production can differ from the sample

A sample is usually made slowly by a sample maker. Bulk production is made by a production line. That shift creates risk: fabric lots can differ, measurements can drift, labels can be placed incorrectly, packaging can be misunderstood, and rush timelines can weaken QC.

The answer is not “trust the sample.” The answer is to turn the sample into a production standard that every line worker, QC person, and packing team can follow.

Sample-to-bulk QC checkpoints

Approve the golden sample or PPS

The approved sample should represent the bulk standard: fabric, color, fit, measurements, trims, logo method, labels, and finish.

Lock fabric and trims

Confirm fabric composition, GSM, stretch, opacity, lining, elastic, zipper, thread, label material, and color before cutting.

Confirm measurements and tolerance

Provide size specs and acceptable tolerance. This matters especially for activewear, bodycon dresses, swimwear, and plus-size apparel.

Hold a pre-production review

Factory teams should review sample, tech pack, difficult sewing steps, label placement, packing method, and buyer comments before line production.

Monitor inline production

Ask for cutting photos, sewing updates, measurement checks, defect notes, and early finished pieces while issues are still fixable.

Inspect before final balance

Check bulk quality, measurements, quantity, size ratio, labels, hangtags, polybags, carton marks, packing list, and photos before shipment.

Risk controls buyers should use

Do this

  • Keep one approved sample as reference.
  • Confirm all spec changes in writing.
  • Ask for inline photos before goods are packed.
  • Inspect before final balance.
  • Plan DDP, FBA, or 3PL rules before packing.

Avoid this

  • Approving bulk from photos only when fit is uncertain.
  • Changing fabric after sample approval.
  • Sending label files after production starts.
  • Waiting until shipment day to discuss carton marks.
  • Paying final balance before QC evidence.

Sample to bulk FAQ

What is a golden sample?

A golden sample is the approved garment that bulk production should match for fabric, fit, measurements, stitching, trims, labels, and finish.

Can I change sizing after bulk production starts?

Major sizing changes after cutting are risky and expensive. Size specs should be confirmed before bulk cutting.

When should I inspect bulk goods?

Ask for inline updates during production and inspect finished goods before paying the final balance and shipping.

What if bulk quality is lower than the sample?

If inspection shows issues, the factory should rework or separate affected pieces before final balance and shipment. Clear written QC standards make this easier.
