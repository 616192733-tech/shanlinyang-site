Custom Activewear Manufacturer: How to Source Quality Gym Wear from China

PUBLISHED 2026-06-22 · SHANLINYANG APPAREL FACTORY TEAM · ~10 MIN READ

The global fitness apparel market is projected to reach over $450 billion by 2028. For entrepreneurs, this growth represents a massive opportunity. But unlike fast fashion, activewear is technical. It requires specialized machinery, specific fabric blends, and rigorous quality control. Finding the right custom activewear manufacturer is the single most important step in building a sustainable brand.

As a leading fitness wear producer in Xiamen, Shanlinyang Apparel has seen the rise of "athleisure" firsthand. In this guide, we'll walk you through the technical side of activewear sourcing and how to ensure your products meet the standards of today's fitness enthusiasts.

1. The Fabric Foundation: Nylon vs. Polyester

When you start your journey with a custom activewear manufacturer, the first question will always be about fabric. In the activewear world, there are two kings:

Nylon/Spandex (Polyamide)

This is the industry standard for high-end yoga wear and compression leggings. Nylon is incredibly soft, moisture-wicking, and has superior stretch recovery. It feels like a "second skin." If you are targeting a premium price point (think Lululemon or Alo Yoga), this is your fabric.

Polyester/Spandex

Polyester is more affordable and takes dye extremely well, making it perfect for sublimated prints or vibrant patterns. It's durable and quick-drying, but it can feel slightly rougher than nylon. It's the go-to for mid-range gym wear and team uniforms.

At Shanlinyang, we offer both, with weights ranging from 220GSM to 350GSM to ensure maximum opacity and "squat-proof" confidence.

2. The Technical Edge: Seamless vs. Cut-and-Sew

Understanding production methods will help you communicate with your custom activewear manufacturer more effectively:

  • Seamless: These are knitted on circular machines, resulting in garments with no side seams. They offer incredible comfort but have limitations in design and color blocking.
  • Cut-and-Sew (Flatlock Stitching): This is our specialty. We cut patterns from rolls of performance fabric and use 4-needle 6-thread flatlock machines. This creates flat, durable seams that don't chafe during intense workouts.

3. Branding Your Fitness Label

Branding in activewear needs to be as durable as the garment itself. Common techniques include:

  • Heat Transfer Logos: Perfect for high-elasticity areas. We use premium silicone transfers that won't crack or peel.
  • Reflective Printing: A must-have for outdoor running gear.
  • Custom Waistbands: Jacquard woven elastic bands can add a high-end, designer feel to leggings and shorts.
  • Private Labels: We offer "tagless" solutions with heat-pressed size and care instructions inside the waistband.

5. Quality Control: The Squat Test and Beyond

Activewear failures are public and embarrassing. To protect your brand, we implement a multi-stage QC process:

  1. Fabric Lab Testing: Checking for colorfastness, shrinkage, and pilling.
  2. The Stretch Test: Ensuring seams hold up under extreme tension.
  3. Opacity Check: The "squat test" to ensure leggings aren't see-through when stretched.
  4. Sizing Audit: Measuring every batch against the master tech pack to ensure consistent fit.

FAQ: Sourcing Custom Activewear

Can you replicate a specific brand's fit?

Yes. If you send us a physical sample of a fit you love, our pattern makers can create a 'master fit' tech pack that replicates those dimensions exactly.

How do I choose the right GSM?

GSM stands for Grams per Square Meter. For leggings, we recommend 280-320 GSM for a premium, non-see-through feel. For summer tops, 160-200 GSM is ideal.

Do you offer eco-friendly fabrics?

Yes, we source GRS-certified recycled fabrics made from ocean plastic and post-consumer waste.

How much does sampling cost?

Sample fees vary by complexity but generally range from $50 to $150 per style. This is fully refundable against your bulk order.

How do you handle sizing for different markets?

We have standardized size charts for US, EU, and AU markets. We can also produce plus-size activewear up to 5XL.
