GUIDE — SWIMWEAR PRODUCTION

Private Label Swimwear Manufacturer: Launch Your Beach Brand from 100 Pieces

The swimwear industry is a lucrative niche with high margins and strong seasonal demand. Launching a beachwear brand is more accessible than ever with the help of a private label swimwear manufacturer.

Swimwear manufacturing is a specialized craft. The garments are small but complex, requiring precision sewing and technical fabrics that can withstand sun, salt, and chlorine. At Shanlinyang Apparel, we specialize in high-quality swimwear for boutique labels. This guide explores how to find the right partner and what to look for in the production process.

1. Why Choose Private Label for Swimwear?

For many startups, "Private Label" is the fastest way to market. A private label swimwear manufacturer like Shanlinyang maintains a catalog of proven, trend-driven styles—from classic one-pieces to high-waisted bikinis. By selecting these designs and adding your custom branding, you skip the expensive and time-consuming "pattern making" and "prototyping" phases.

This allows you to focus your budget on marketing and building your brand community, while still offering a product that fits perfectly and looks premium.

2. Selecting the Right Swimwear Fabrics

Quality swimwear starts with the fabric. The industry standard is a blend of **80% Nylon and 20% Spandex**. Nylon provides the soft, luxurious feel, while Spandex provides the "snap" and shape retention.

When sourcing, ask for "high-gauge" knits which are less likely to become see-through when wet. You should also consider textured fabrics like ribbed or crinkle knits, which are currently trending and offer a unique tactile feel.

3. Technical Construction Details

Swimwear requires specialized machines, such as the **4-needle 6-thread flatlock machine**, to create seams that are strong yet elastic. Check the samples for:

  • Lining: High-quality swimwear should be fully lined (usually with a lighter-weight nylon/spandex blend) for comfort and opacity.
  • Elastication: The elastic used in the leg openings and straps must be chlorine-resistant to prevent "bagging" over time.
  • Removable Padding: Most women prefer the option of removable cups for shaping and support.

4. Customization and Branding

Branding is what separates a "generic swimsuit" from your "brand's product." Even with private label styles, you can customize:

  • Heat-Transfer Labels: These are printed directly onto the fabric, replacing itchy woven tags—ideal for swimwear.
  • Hang Tags: Use premium cardstock with your brand's story.
  • Custom Hardware: Consider adding gold, silver, or matte black rings, sliders, or brand-etched toggles.

5. The Rise of Sustainable Swimwear

Sustainability is particularly important in the swimwear world, given its connection to the ocean. Many brands now use recycled fabrics like **ECONYL®** or **Repreve**. These fabrics are made from recycled plastics and are indistinguishable from "virgin" nylon in terms of performance and feel. Offering sustainable options is a great way to differentiate your brand in a crowded market.

6. MOQs and Scaling Your Collection

One of the biggest hurdles for new brands is the high Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) of many large factories. Finding a partner like Shanlinyang that offers **low MOQs of 100 pieces per style** is vital. This allows you to launch a collection with multiple styles and colors without over-committing your capital to inventory.
