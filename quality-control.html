CAPABILITY — QUALITY CONTROL

Three checkpoints between fabric and your warehouse

Every order — 2 pieces or 2,000 — passes the same three-stage inspection, documented with photos you approve before anything ships.

  1. Incoming fabric inspection

    Color against approved lab dip, weight (gsm) against spec, defect scan under light table. Wrong dye lots are rejected before cutting — the cheapest place to catch a problem.

  2. Inline sewing QC

    Roving inspectors on the line check seam strength, stitch density, logo placement and label position while garments are being sewn — plus needle detection on every knit order.

  3. Final AQL inspection

    AQL 2.5 sampling on finished goods: points of measure against the spec sheet (±1–1.5 cm), appearance, packaging and barcodes. You receive a photographed report for sign-off before balance payment.

QC FAQ

FAQ

Can I send my own inspector?
Yes — third-party inspection by SGS, TÜV or your own agent is welcome at any checkpoint, on any order size.
What happens if QC finds defects?
Defective units are repaired or remade before shipment at our cost; the photographed final report goes to you for approval before balance payment.
What measurement tolerance do you work to?
±1–1.5 cm on key points of measure, agreed on the size spec sheet before bulk cutting.
