Amazon FBA Clothing Supplier in China: Private Label Guide for Sellers

Selling clothing on Amazon via the FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) program is one of the most profitable but also one of the most challenging e-commerce models. The competition is fierce, and Amazon's customer-centric policies mean that you must provide high-quality products and accurate sizing to survive. A single bad batch of garments can lead to a surge in returns, negative reviews, and ultimately, the suspension of your selling account.

In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of finding a reliable Amazon FBA clothing supplier in China, managing the unique requirements of the platform, and building a sustainable private label brand.

Why Your Choice of Supplier Determines Your Amazon Success

On Amazon, the "flywheel" effect is driven by reviews and sales velocity. If your garments are poorly made or if the sizing doesn't match the US or European standards, your return rate will skyrocket. Amazon tracks return rates closely, and high rates will hurt your organic ranking. Therefore, you cannot afford to cut corners on manufacturing.

You need a supplier that understands Western sizing and has a robust internal quality control process. At Shanlinyang, we work with many successful Amazon sellers. We provide them with detailed size charts, high-resolution product photos for their listings, and consistent quality that keeps their "Voice of the Customer" metrics in the green.

Amazon's Strict Packaging and Labeling Requirements

Shipping to an Amazon warehouse is not like shipping to a traditional retail store. Amazon has very specific rules that your supplier must follow. Each individual garment must be in its own transparent poly bag. Each bag must have a suffocation warning (if the opening is over 5 inches) and a scannable FNSKU barcode.

If your supplier makes a mistake here—for example, if the barcodes are blurry or if they are placed over a seam—Amazon may reject the entire shipment or charge you "prep fees" to fix it. When choosing a supplier, ask if they are familiar with "Amazon Prep" services. A reliable partner will handle the barcode application and poly-bagging for you as part of their standard service.

The Importance of "Squat-Proof" and Accurate Sizing

If you are selling activewear or leggings on Amazon, "squat-proof" is the most important term you need to know. Amazon customers are notorious for leaving 1-star reviews if their leggings are transparent during a workout. To avoid this, you must choose a fabric with a high enough GSM (typically 280-320) and a double-knit construction.

Similarly, "True to Size" is a major selling point. Many Chinese factories use Asian sizing, which is significantly smaller than Western sizing. You must provide your manufacturer with a detailed size chart based on your target market (e.g., US Standard Sizing). Don't just rely on the factory's "standard" sizes—always verify the measurements with samples before you go to bulk production.

Managing Quality Control with Third-Party Inspections

Even if you trust your supplier, we always recommend hiring a third-party inspection company (like V-Trust or QIMA) to visit the factory before the final shipment is sent. This is especially important for Amazon sellers because once the goods are in Amazon's warehouse, you have no way to fix a defect without paying expensive "removal fees."

The inspector will check a random sample of your order for sewing defects, measurement accuracy, and proper labeling. This "pre-shipment inspection" is your last line of defense. At Shanlinyang, we welcome third-party inspectors and provide them with the space and cooperation they need to ensure our clients are 100% satisfied with their order.

Navigating Amazon's Brand Registry and Private Labeling

To truly succeed on Amazon, you need to enroll in Amazon Brand Registry. This gives you access to "A+ Content" (enhanced descriptions), a brand storefront, and better protection against "hijackers" (other sellers trying to sell on your listing). To enroll, you need a registered trademark and a product that is clearly branded.

This is where private labeling comes in. Your manufacturer should be able to sew in your brand label, add a custom hang tag, and even provide branded poly bags. These physical branding elements are proof to Amazon that you are a legitimate brand owner. A professional manufacturer will help you create these elements to ensure they meet both Amazon's requirements and your brand's aesthetic.

Logistics: Shipping from China to Amazon FBA

You have two main options for shipping to FBA: Direct Shipping or using a Third-Party Logistics (3PL) provider. Direct shipping is faster and cheaper, as your manufacturer sends the goods straight to the Amazon warehouse. However, it leaves no room for error. If the shipment is rejected, you have no local warehouse to send it to for fixing.

Using a 3PL in the US or Europe provides a safety net. The goods are shipped to the 3PL first, where they are inspected and then forwarded to Amazon in smaller batches. This allows you to manage your inventory levels better and avoid Amazon's "long-term storage fees." Your manufacturer should be flexible enough to work with whichever logistics strategy you choose.
