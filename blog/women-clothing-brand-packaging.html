GUIDE — BRANDING & PACKAGING

Custom Clothing Packaging: Tags, Labels & Boxes from Your Manufacturer

In the world of fashion, the unboxing experience is just as important as the garment itself. Custom packaging is the final touch that transforms a piece of clothing into a branded product.

When you work with a custom clothing packaging manufacturer, you aren't just buying bags and boxes; you're building a brand identity. Many new brand owners assume they need to source packaging separately, but professional factories like Shanlinyang Apparel can manage the entire process for you. This guide explores the different types of custom packaging available and how to source them efficiently from your manufacturer.

1. Why Source Packaging via Your Clothing Manufacturer?

Sourcing packaging directly through your clothing manufacturer offers several logistical advantages. First, it ensures that your tags and labels are applied correctly during the production process. Second, it saves on shipping costs, as the packaging and garments are packed and shipped together as a finished product.

Most importantly, it simplifies your supply chain. Instead of managing three different vendors for labels, bags, and sewing, you have one point of contact who ensures everything matches your brand's aesthetic and quality standards.

2. Custom Woven Labels and Care Tags

Woven labels are the "DNA" of your garment. They typically include your brand logo and are sewn into the neckline or side seam. For a premium feel, consider "high-definition" weaving which allows for finer detail.

Care tags, often printed on satin or nylon, are a legal requirement in most countries. They must include fabric composition, country of origin, and washing instructions. Your manufacturer can help you draft these according to US or EU regulations to ensure compliance.

3. Hang Tags: The Silent Salesman

Hang tags are the most visible form of packaging before a garment is worn. They provide space for branding, pricing, and storytelling. Common options include:

  • Cardstock: Matte, glossy, or textured finishes.
  • Special Techniques: Foil stamping, embossing, or UV coating.
  • Attachments: Waxed cotton cords, plastic seals, or safety pins.

A well-designed hang tag elevates the perceived value of your item, making it look at home in a high-end boutique or a premium online store.

4. Branded Poly Bags and Sustainable Alternatives

For shipping and protection, every garment is usually placed in a poly bag. Customizing these with your logo or a "thank you" message adds a professional touch.

In 2025, sustainability is a major trend. Many brands are moving away from traditional plastic and opting for compostable bags made from cornstarch or 100% recycled LDPE. Ask your manufacturer about eco-friendly options to align with your brand's environmental values.

5. Mailer Boxes and Shipping Bags

If you are a DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) brand, your shipping packaging is the first thing your customer sees. Custom mailer boxes provide maximum protection and a premium unboxing feel, though they are more expensive to ship.

Custom poly mailers (shipping bags) are a popular alternative for apparel because they are lightweight and tear-resistant. They can be printed with full-color designs to make your package stand out in the mail.

6. Developing Your Packaging Strategy

When planning your packaging, start with the essentials: main labels, care tags, and poly bags. As your brand grows, you can add more elaborate elements like tissue paper, stickers, and custom boxes. Always request samples of the packaging materials before committing to a large run to ensure the colors and textures are exactly what you envisioned.
