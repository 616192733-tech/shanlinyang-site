Custom Clothing Labels: Types, Costs & How to Order from China

Branding is much more than just a logo on a website. It's the physical experience your customer has when they touch your garment. One of the most effective ways to elevate your brand and create a professional image is through custom clothing labels. A well-designed label doesn't just provide necessary care information; it serves as a lasting reminder of your brand's quality and identity.

In this guide, we'll explore the different types of clothing labels available, the costs involved in sourcing them from China, and the step-by-step process of ordering them for your next collection.

The Different Types of Custom Clothing Labels

When it comes to labels, you have several options depending on your garment type and brand aesthetic. The most common is the Woven Label. These are made by weaving polyester threads together on a loom to create your design. They are durable, professional, and won't fade in the wash. Woven labels come in different finishes like Damask (high detail), Satin (shiny), and Taffeta (budget-friendly).

For activewear or items where comfort is paramount, Heat Transfer Labels (also known as "tagless" labels) are a popular choice. These are printed onto a carrier sheet and then heat-pressed onto the inside of the garment. They eliminate the risk of irritation from a physical tag, which is a major benefit for gym wear. Other options include Printed Labels (often cotton or satin) and Rubber/Silicone Labels for a modern, 3D look.

Understanding Care Labels and Legal Requirements

Beyond branding, labels also serve a legal purpose. Most countries have strict regulations regarding the information that must be present on a garment. This usually includes the Fiber Content (e.g., 90% Nylon, 10% Spandex), the Country of Origin (e.g., Made in China), and Care Instructions (e.g., Machine wash cold, do not bleach).

Failure to include this information correctly can lead to your products being seized at customs or even legal action. At Shanlinyang, we stay up-to-date with international labeling requirements to ensure your products are compliant for sale in the US, Europe, and beyond. We can help you design a care label that is both functional and aesthetically pleasing, often combining it with your main brand label to save costs.

Hang Tags: The Finishing Touch

While the internal labels are for the long term, Hang Tags are all about the initial presentation. They provide a space for your brand story, pricing, and social media handles. Hang tags are typically made from cardstock and can be customized with various finishes, such as Matte/Gloss Lamination, Spot UV, Foil Stamping, or Embossing.

The string or ribbon used to attach the hang tag also offers a branding opportunity. You can choose from simple cotton string, luxury ribbon, or even custom-molded plastic seals with your logo. While hang tags are often discarded by the customer after purchase, they play a huge role in the perceived value of the item when it's being displayed or unboxed.

The Cost of Sourcing Labels from China

One of the biggest advantages of sourcing labels from China is the incredibly low per-unit cost. For a standard woven label, you might pay between $0.02 and $0.10 depending on the quantity and complexity. Hang tags are similarly affordable. The main "cost" for small brands is often the Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ), which is usually around 500 to 1,000 pieces per design.

However, because labels are small and lightweight, the shipping costs are negligible. We often recommend that our clients order a large batch of labels (e.g., 5,000 pieces) all at once. This drives the unit price down significantly, and the labels can be stored and used over multiple production runs. This is a cost-effective way to maintain branding consistency as your brand grows.

How to Order Custom Labels: A Step-by-Step Guide

The process of ordering labels is straightforward if you have the right design files. Here's how it usually works:

  1. Design Your Label: Create your artwork in a vector format (AI, EPS, or PDF). Make sure all fonts are outlined and specify your Pantone colors for accuracy.
  2. Choose Your Type and Finish: Decide between woven, printed, or heat transfer based on your garment type. Choose the fold type (e.g., center fold, end fold, or loop fold).
  3. Request a Sample: Most label factories will provide a digital mockup or a physical sample for a small fee. This allows you to check the thread colors and weaving quality before bulk production.
  4. Production and Shipping: Once approved, production typically takes 7-10 days. The labels can then be shipped directly to your garment manufacturer for application.

Working with a Full-Service Manufacturer

While you can certainly source your labels independently, there are many benefits to letting your garment manufacturer handle it. At Shanlinyang, we work with a network of trusted label and trim suppliers. When we handle your labels, we ensure that they are delivered on time for the sewing stage, and we take responsibility for the quality control of the labels themselves.

This "one-stop-shop" approach reduces your workload and minimizes the risk of miscommunication between different vendors. We can coordinate the colors of your labels, zippers, and drawstrings to ensure a perfectly cohesive look for your entire collection.
