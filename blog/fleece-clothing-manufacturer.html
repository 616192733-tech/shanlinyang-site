Choosing the Right Fabric Weight (GSM) for Your Fleece Line

One of the most important decisions when working with a fleece clothing manufacturer is selecting the fabric weight, measured in grams per square meter (GSM). For lightweight spring pullovers, a 200-240 GSM fleece is often sufficient. However, for that iconic "heavyweight" hoodie feel popular in streetwear today, brands usually opt for 350-450 GSM or even higher.

The GSM doesn't just affect warmth; it changes the drape and silhouette of the garment. Heavier fabrics hold their shape better, providing that structured, boxy look, while lighter fabrics are better for draped, relaxed styles. We provide fabric swatches for all our clients to ensure they can feel the difference before committing to bulk production.

Technical Considerations in Fleece Production

Sewing fleece requires specialized machinery and skill. Because the fabric is thick and stretchy, standard sewing techniques can lead to puckering or uneven seams. We utilize flatlock stitching and reinforced coverstitching to ensure seams are flat, comfortable against the skin, and strong enough to withstand repeated wear and washing. This attention to technical detail is what separates a professional manufacturer from a general garment factory.

Quality control is also vital. Fleece can be prone to pilling if the fabric quality is low. We source high-quality "anti-pilling" fleece to ensure your customers' garments look new for longer. Every batch of fabric is tested for colorfastness and shrinkage before it reaches the cutting table, minimizing defects in the final product.
