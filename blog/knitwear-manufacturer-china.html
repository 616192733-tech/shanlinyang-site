Understanding Knitwear Gauges (GG)

In knitwear manufacturing, "gauge" refers to the number of needles per inch on the knitting machine. The lower the gauge number, the "chunkier" the knit. For example, a 3gg or 5gg machine is used for those thick, cozy "grandpa" cardigans and chunky cable-knit sweaters. Conversely, a 12gg or 14gg machine produces a fine, smooth fabric suitable for lightweight turtlenecks and elegant basics.

Selecting the right gauge is essential for achieving the desired look and function of your garment. Our team advises clients on the best gauge for their designs, considering the target price point and the intended season. We can even combine different gauges in a single collection to offer a diverse range of textures and styles.

Design Techniques: Intarsia, Jacquard, and Cable Knits

Modern knitwear is all about detail. Intarsia and jacquard are popular techniques for incorporating complex patterns and logos directly into the knit. While jacquard creates a double-layered fabric with the design visible on the surface, intarsia allows for multi-colored patterns without the extra bulk, making it ideal for lightweight, high-end designs.

Cable knitting remains a timeless classic for winter wear. We can produce a variety of cable patterns, from simple twists to intricate "honeycomb" designs. Our computerized knitting machines allow for high precision, ensuring that patterns are consistent across every piece in your production run. These textures add value and a "handmade" feel to your collection.

The Importance of Finishing in Knitwear

The assembly and finishing of knitwear are what determine its durability and fit. Unlike cut-and-sew garments, high-quality knitwear is often "fully fashioned," meaning the pieces are knitted to shape rather than cut from a flat fabric. These pieces are then "linked" together needle-by-needle. This process creates stronger, cleaner seams that won't fray or come apart.

After assembly, every garment is washed and blocked. This "wet finishing" process sets the dimensions of the sweater and enhances the softness of the yarn. We also perform rigorous quality checks to ensure there are no dropped stitches, uneven tension, or measurement discrepancies. Our attention to these details ensures that your customers receive a product that looks and feels expensive.
