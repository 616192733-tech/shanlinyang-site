How to Start an Activewear Brand with 100 Pieces: Realistic Budget & Timeline

Launching a fitness apparel line no longer requires ordering thousands of units. In 2025, the barrier to entry has lowered significantly. By starting with just 100 pieces, you can test your designs, validate your market, and build your brand without the risk of heavy inventory overhead. This guide breaks down the exact steps, costs, and timeframes involved in starting your activewear brand with a low MOQ of 100 pieces.

1. Why 100 Pieces is the Sweet Spot for Emerging Activewear Brands

In the traditional garment industry, MOQs (Minimum Order Quantities) often start at 500 or 1,000 pieces per style. For a startup, this is a massive gamble. Starting with 100 pieces offers a "Lean Startup" approach to fashion. It allows you to produce a high-quality product while keeping your initial investment manageable.

At 100 pieces, you gain several advantages. First, you reduce your financial risk. If a design doesn't resonate with your audience, you aren't stuck with thousands of unsold units. Second, you can offer more variety. Instead of ordering 500 units of one black legging, you could potentially order 100 units each of five different styles or colors (depending on the factory's flexibility). Third, it creates a sense of exclusivity and scarcity, which can be a powerful marketing tool for a new brand.

Working with a factory like Shanlinyang Apparel that specializes in low MOQs means you don't sacrifice quality for quantity. You still get access to premium fabrics, professional patterns, and rigorous quality control, but scaled to your startup's needs.

2. Understanding the Budget: What Does it Actually Cost?

One of the most frequent questions we get is: "How much money do I actually need?" To start an activewear brand with 100 pieces, you should have a realistic budget of $3,000 to $7,000. Here is how that budget is typically allocated:

  • Sampling ($300 - $800): Expect to pay for 1-2 rounds of samples. While sample costs are often 2x-3x the bulk price, this step is non-negotiable for ensuring fit and quality.
  • Bulk Production ($1,500 - $3,500): For 100 premium leggings or sets, the cost per unit typically ranges from $15 to $35 depending on the complexity and fabric.
  • Shipping and Customs ($400 - $1,000): Shipping 100 pieces by air is fast (7 days) but more expensive than sea. Don't forget to account for import duties in your country.
  • Branding and Packaging ($200 - $500): Custom labels, hangtags, and eco-friendly mailers add that professional touch.
  • Marketing and Website ($500 - $1,200): Shopify fees, a basic theme, and some initial social media ad spend or influencer seeding.

By budgeting correctly from the start, you avoid the "hidden costs" that often catch new founders off guard.

3. The Timeline: From Concept to Your First Sale in 90 Days

Time is money. A typical production cycle for a 100-piece order takes about 3 months. Here is a realistic breakdown of that 90-day timeline:

  • Day 1-15: Design & Tech Packs. Finalize your designs and create tech packs (detailed blueprints for your garments). If you don't have tech packs, a good factory can help you create them based on reference samples.
  • Day 16-45: Sampling Phase. The factory produces your first sample. You receive it, test the fit and fabric (the "squat test" is essential for leggings!), and provide feedback. If needed, a second "pre-production" sample is made.
  • Day 46-75: Bulk Production. Once you approve the sample, bulk production begins. This involves fabric cutting, sewing, quality inspections, and packaging.
  • Day 76-90: Shipping & Launch. Your goods are shipped via air courier. While they are in transit, you finalize your website and marketing materials so you can start selling the moment they arrive.

4. Sourcing Your Fabric and Factory for Low MOQ Production

Not every factory is set up for 100-piece orders. Large factories often find small orders disruptive to their production lines. You need to find a partner that views small brands as long-term partners, not just "small fry."

When sourcing for activewear, fabric is everything. You want a four-way stretch, moisture-wicking material that isn't see-through. For 100 pieces, you might be limited to "available colors" in the market rather than custom-dyed fabrics (which usually require 500+ meters). However, high-quality factories maintain a wide stock of premium fabrics in popular colors like black, navy, forest green, and mauve.

Ask potential manufacturers about their experience with activewear. Do they understand flatlock stitching? Do they have specialized machines for high-stretch fabrics? At Shanlinyang, we use the same high-tech equipment for our 100-piece orders as we do for our larger clients, ensuring your startup brand looks professional from day one.

5. Marketing Your First 100 Pieces: High-Impact Strategies for Startups

With only 100 pieces, you can't afford a broad, expensive marketing campaign. You need a surgical approach. Your goal is to build a community, not just a customer base.

Leverage Micro-Influencers: Send 10-20% of your stock to micro-influencers in the fitness and yoga niche. Their engagement rates are often higher than celebrities, and their endorsement carries more trust. If 10 influencers each convince 5 people to buy, you've already sold half your stock.

Focus on User-Generated Content (UGC): Encourage your first customers to post photos. This provides social proof and creates a feedback loop for your next collection. High-quality imagery is crucial; your 100 pieces need to look like they belong in a high-end boutique.

Pre-Launch Email List: Start building an email list months before you launch. Offer a "early bird" discount or exclusive access. By the time your 100 pieces arrive, you should have at least 500 people on your list ready to buy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it really possible to start an activewear brand with only 100 pieces?

Yes, it is entirely possible. Many Chinese manufacturers, including Shanlinyang Apparel, offer low MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity) options starting at 100 pieces per style/color to help new brands enter the market without a massive upfront investment.

What is a realistic budget for a 100-piece activewear launch?

For 100 pieces, you should budget between $3,000 to $7,000. This includes product development, sampling, production, shipping, and initial marketing. Costs vary based on fabric choice and design complexity.

How long does the production process take for a low MOQ order?

Typically, the process takes 60 to 90 days. This includes 2-4 weeks for sampling and 4-6 weeks for bulk production, plus shipping time (7 days by air or 30+ days by sea).

Can I get my own labels and tags with only 100 pieces?

Yes, private labeling is available even for 100 pieces. While some labels might have a higher MOQ (like 500 or 1000), we can store the extras for your future orders, allowing your current 100 pieces to be fully branded.

What are the best fabrics for a startup activewear brand?

For startups, we recommend versatile fabrics like Nylon/Spandex blends (73/27 or 80/20) for leggings and Polyester/Spandex for tops. These are durable, high-performance, and readily available in various colors.
