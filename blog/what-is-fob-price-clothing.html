What Is FOB Price in Clothing Manufacturing? Everything Brands Need to Know

If you've requested a quote from a Chinese factory, you've likely seen the term "FOB." But what is FOB price clothing manufacturer really referring to? Understanding Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) is vital for fashion brands to avoid unexpected shipping bills and legal disputes. This guide breaks down exactly what FOB means, what is included in the price, and why it is the "gold standard" for garment sourcing.

In the world of international trade, clarity is currency. Knowing where the factory's responsibility ends and yours begins is the difference between a smooth launch and a logistics nightmare. At Shanlinyang Apparel, we provide FOB Xiamen pricing as our standard. Here is everything you need to know about this crucial term.

1. The Definition: What Does FOB Actually Mean?

FOB stands for **Free on Board** (sometimes referred to as Freight on Board). In clothing manufacturing, it means that the manufacturer is responsible for the costs and risks of the goods until they are loaded onto a ship or plane at a specific port.

Once the goods pass the ship's rail (or are scanned into the air freight warehouse) at the port of origin, the ownership and the risk of loss transfer to you, the buyer.

2. What Is Included in an FOB Price Quote?

When you see a quote like "$8.50 FOB Xiamen," that price includes more than just the fabric and labor. It covers:

  • The Garment Cost: Fabric, trims, CMT (Cut, Make, Trim) labor.
  • Packaging: Poly-bags, hangtags, and export-grade master cartons.
  • Local Logistics: Trucking from our factory in Xiamen to the Xiamen Port.
  • Export Clearance: Preparing the Chinese customs documents and paying the local export fees.
What is NOT included: International ocean/air freight, marine insurance, import duties in your country, and local delivery from your port to your warehouse.

3. FOB vs. EXW (Ex-Works): Which Is Better?

New brands often compare FOB with EXW.

  • **EXW (Ex-Works):** The factory just makes the goods and leaves them at their door. You have to arrange a truck to pick them up and handle all Chinese export customs. This is often difficult for foreigners without a Chinese logistics partner.
  • **FOB (Free on Board):** The factory handles the "first mile" and the complex Chinese paperwork. This is highly recommended for 95% of boutique brands and online stores.

4. FOB vs. CIF: Understanding the Risk

CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight) means the factory pays for the shipping and insurance to your home port. While this sounds easier, it has downsides:

  • Hidden Markups: Factories often add a margin to the shipping cost.
  • Lack of Control: You can't choose the shipping line or the arrival date.
  • DTHC (Destination Terminal Handling Charges): CIF quotes often look cheap, but you may get hit with massive "handling fees" once the ship arrives at your port.
By choosing **FOB**, you can use your own freight forwarder, giving you full control over the shipping speed and the final cost.

5. Why "FOB Xiamen" or "FOB Guangzhou" Matters

The port named after the "FOB" is critical. If your freight forwarder is based in Ningbo but your factory quotes "FOB Xiamen," you will have to pay for the trucking between those two cities. Always ensure your factory is quoting from the port nearest to their production facility to keep local costs as low as possible.

6. Key Questions to Ask Your Manufacturer About Pricing

To ensure you are getting a true FOB quote, ask these questions:

  • "Does this price include the cost of export-grade master cartons?"
  • "Is the China export clearance fee included?"
  • "Which specific port are you quoting from (e.g., Xiamen, Shenzhen, or Shanghai)?"
  • "Can you provide the estimated carton dimensions and weight so I can get a freight quote?"

People Also Ask (FAQ)

What does FOB mean in clothing manufacturing?

FOB stands for 'Free on Board.' It means the manufacturer's price includes the cost of the goods and the transport of those goods to a specific port (like Xiamen or Shanghai). The buyer is responsible for the cost of transport from that port to the final destination.

What is included in a FOB price?

FOB price includes fabric, labor, labels, packaging, and the local trucking and export clearance fees to get the goods onto a ship or plane at the designated port.

Is FOB better than EXW for beginners?

Yes. FOB is usually better because the factory handles the local Chinese export paperwork and trucking. With EXW (Ex-Works), the buyer must arrange for someone to pick up the goods from the factory door, which can be complex for new brands.

What is the difference between FOB and CIF?

Under FOB, the buyer pays for the main international freight and insurance. Under CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight), the seller pays to get the goods all the way to the destination port.

Can I negotiate FOB prices?

Yes, FOB prices are highly negotiable based on order volume. A higher MOQ allows the factory to spread the fixed costs (like local trucking and export documents) over more units, lowering the price.
