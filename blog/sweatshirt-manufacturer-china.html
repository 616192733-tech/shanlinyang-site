Selecting the Ideal Fabric for Your Sweatshirt Line

The choice of fabric is the most critical decision in sweatshirt manufacturing. The two industry standards are French terry and fleece. French terry is characterized by its looped interior, making it more breathable and suitable for layering or milder climates. Fleece is brushed on the inside for a soft, fuzzy feel that provides extra warmth, making it the preferred choice for winter collections.

We source these fabrics in a range of weights. For a premium, high-end feel, we recommend a heavyweight fabric of 380 GSM or higher. This weight provides the "substance" that customers associate with luxury streetwear. We also offer organic cotton and recycled polyester blends for brands with a focus on sustainability, ensuring your products are as ethical as they are stylish.

Decoration and Branding: Making Your Sweatshirt Unique

A blank sweatshirt is a versatile canvas for branding. We offer a wide variety of decoration techniques to help your brand stand out. Beyond the main design, we provide complete private labeling services. We can produce custom neck labels (either woven or heat-transferred for a "tagless" feel), hem tags, and branded hangtags. The Technical Side of Sweatshirt Production

Quality construction is what makes a sweatshirt last. We use reinforced stitching on high-stress areas like the neckline and armholes to prevent fraying and ensure longevity. Our ribbing is sourced for its "recovery" properties, meaning the cuffs and waistbands won't lose their shape after repeated wear and washing. This attention to detail is a hallmark of our manufacturing process.

Durability also comes from the fabric treatment. We use anti-pilling and pre-shrinking processes to ensure that your sweatshirts look new for longer and maintain their size after the first wash. Every batch of garments undergoes a multi-stage quality control inspection, checking for everything from color accuracy to the integrity of the prints and embroidery.
