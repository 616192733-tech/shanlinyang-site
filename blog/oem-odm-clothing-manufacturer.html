OEM vs ODM Clothing Manufacturing: Which Model Is Right for Your Brand?

PUBLISHED 2026-06-22 · SHANLINYANG APPAREL FACTORY TEAM · ~9 MIN READ

When you start searching for a clothing manufacturer, you'll immediately encounter two terms: OEM and ODM. While they may sound like technical jargon, understanding the difference between these two manufacturing models is critical. Your choice will determine your production costs, your time-to-market, and the level of control you have over your final product.

At Shanlinyang Apparel, we operate as both an OEM and ODM clothing manufacturer. This gives us a unique perspective on which model works best for different types of fashion businesses. In this guide, we'll strip away the complexity and help you decide which path is right for your brand's current stage.

What is OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturing)?

In the OEM model, the brand (you) is the designer and the architect. You provide the factory with a "Tech Pack" that includes:

  • Detailed sketches and fit requirements.
  • Specific fabric compositions and weights.
  • Exact measurements for every size.
  • Branding placement (labels, logos, buttons).

The OEM clothing manufacturer acts solely as the producer. They follow your blueprint to the letter. This is the gold standard for established brands that have a very specific "signature fit" or unique design DNA that can't be found elsewhere.

What is ODM (Original Design Manufacturing)?

ODM is often called "Private Label" or "White Label" manufacturing. In this model, the factory is the designer. We create a collection of trendy, high-quality garments based on current market data and fashion forecasts. You then:

  • Browse our catalog of two-piece sets, activewear, or dresses.
  • Select the styles you like.
  • Request minor modifications (e.g., "Make this dress in Sage Green instead of Pink").
  • Add your custom labels, hangtags, and packaging.

As an ODM clothing manufacturer, we provide the solution, and you provide the brand identity. This is the fastest and most cost-effective way to launch or expand a clothing line.

Which Model Should You Choose?

Choose OEM If:

  • You have a unique design that is currently missing from the market.
  • You have a specific sizing standard (e.g., a specialist plus-size brand with custom fit requirements).
  • You have the budget to invest in technical design and multiple rounds of sampling.
  • You want full ownership of your product's intellectual property.

Choose ODM If:

  • You are a startup or a boutique owner looking to launch quickly.
  • You want to test new trends without a massive financial risk.
  • You don't have a technical design background or an in-house designer.
  • You want to focus on marketing and community building rather than production logistics.

The Shanlinyang Hybrid Approach

Many of our most successful clients use a hybrid strategy. They use our ODM services for their core, "basics" collection to ensure consistent quality and fast restocking. Meanwhile, they use our OEM services for their "hero items"—the unique, complex designs that define their brand's aesthetic.

FAQ: OEM and ODM Manufacturing

Can I use your ODM designs but change the fabric?

Yes. This is a very common request. We can take one of our existing patterns and produce it in a fabric from our library that better suits your brand's price point or aesthetic.

Does Shanlinyang provide tech pack design services?

While we prefer you provide a tech pack for OEM, our in-house team can help refine your sketches and create professional tech packs for a nominal fee.

What is the minimum for a full OEM order?

For full custom OEM orders, we typically look for 300 pieces per style. However, for simpler designs, we may be able to start at 200 pieces.

Are my OEM designs safe with you?

Yes. We value our long-term partnerships. We are happy to sign an NDA to guarantee that your custom designs remain exclusive to your brand.

How do I see your ODM catalog?

You can browse our core lines on our products page or contact us directly to receive our latest seasonal PDF catalogs.
