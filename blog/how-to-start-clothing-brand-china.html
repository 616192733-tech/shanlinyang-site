How to Start a Clothing Brand with a Chinese Manufacturer (Step-by-Step)

PUBLISHED 2026-06-23 · SHANLINYANG APPAREL FACTORY TEAM · ~15 MIN READ

The dream of seeing your own designs on the runway or in the hands of happy customers is powerful. But for many aspiring fashion entrepreneurs, the gap between a creative idea and a physical product feels like a mountain. China remains the world's most efficient hub for apparel production, offering an unmatched combination of speed, quality, and technical expertise. However, knowing how to start a clothing brand with a manufacturer in China requires more than just a Google search; it requires a strategic roadmap.

At Shanlinyang Apparel, we have helped hundreds of brands—from boutique owners to global DTC labels—navigate the complexities of overseas production. This guide breaks down the process into actionable steps to ensure your first collection is a success.

1. Concept and Design: The Birth of Your Brand

Before you talk to a clothing manufacturer, you need a clear vision. Don't worry if you can't draw like a professional artist; you just need to communicate your ideas effectively.

  • Mood Boards: Collect images that represent the vibe of your brand. Look at colors, textures, fits, and lifestyle shots. Tools like Pinterest are perfect for this.
  • Inspiration Samples: Find existing garments that have the fit or fabric you love. One of the best ways to work with a Chinese factory is to send a physical sample and say, "I want this fit, but with these changes."
  • The Tech Pack: This is the most critical document. A tech pack is a blueprint for your garment. it includes technical drawings, measurements (size charts), fabric specifications, and branding details. If you don't have one, a full-service factory like Shanlinyang can help you create it.

2. Finding and Vetting the Right Factory

Not all factories are created equal. The key to knowing how to start a clothing brand with a manufacturer in China is finding a partner that matches your scale and style.

Factory vs. Trading Company: A factory owns the machines and employs the workers. A trading company acts as a middleman. For a startup, a high-quality trading company with strong factory partnerships can often provide better communication and lower MOQs. For larger brands, going direct-to-factory is more cost-effective.

Niche Expertise: Don't ask a winter coat factory to make swimwear. Look for specialists. Shanlinyang specializes in activewear, dresses, and women's fashion sets. Specialist factories have the right machinery and a deep understanding of the specific fabrics needed for those styles.

3. The Sampling Phase: Turning Vision into Reality

Sampling is where the "magic" happens. It’s the stage where you test the factory's capability and the garment's fit. Never skip this step.

  • Proto Sample: This is the first version made from "available" fabric to check the fit and construction.
  • Salesman Sample (SMS): Made with the actual fabric and colors you intend to sell. Use these for your photoshoot and marketing.
  • Pre-Production Sample (PPS): The final "gold standard." Once you approve this, bulk production begins.

Expect to pay a premium for samples (usually 2-3x the bulk price). This is normal as the factory has to set up machines for just one or two units.

4. Sourcing Fabrics and Trims

The feel of your clothing is determined by the fabric. In China, we have access to the world's largest fabric markets. When working with a clothing manufacturer, you'll need to decide between:

Stock Fabrics: Fabrics that are already produced and available in small quantities. This is best for low MOQs and fast turnarounds.

Custom Fabrics: If you need a specific weight, blend, or a custom-dyed color (Pantone matched), the factory will need to order from the mill. This usually requires an MOQ of 500-1,000 meters per color.

At Shanlinyang, we maintain an extensive fabric library with everything from high-performance nylon/spandex to eco-friendly recycled polyesters, helping you find the perfect balance between quality and cost.

5. Bulk Production and Quality Control

Once samples are approved and a deposit is paid, bulk production starts. This is where most brand owners feel the most stress, but a good partner keeps you informed.

Production Timeline: A typical bulk order takes 30-45 days. This includes fabric procurement, cutting, sewing, and finishing.

QC Inspections: A reputable factory has an in-house QC team. However, we also encourage third-party inspections (like SGS or V-Trust) for your first few orders. They will check for:

  • Stitching consistency and durability.
  • Sizing accuracy across the whole batch.
  • Proper labeling and packaging.
  • Functionality (zippers, buttons, drawstrings).

6. Logistics: Getting Your Goods Home

The final step in how to start a clothing brand with a manufacturer in China is shipping. You have three main options:

  • Air Express (FedEx/DHL/UPS): Fastest (5-7 days) but most expensive. Best for small orders or urgent restocks.
  • Air Freight: Moderate speed (10-15 days) and cost. Good for mid-sized orders.
  • Sea Freight: Slowest (30-45 days) but most economical for large volumes.

For many online brands, we recommend DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) shipping. It’s a "hands-off" service where we handle everything from the factory floor to your warehouse door, including customs clearance and duties.

FAQ: Starting a Clothing Brand

What if my sample isn't perfect the first time?

It rarely is! Sampling is an iterative process. We provide one round of revisions based on your feedback to ensure the bulk production matches your vision exactly.

Do you offer sustainable fabric options?

Yes. We have a growing selection of GRS-certified recycled fabrics, organic cotton, and bamboo blends for eco-conscious brands.

How do I pay a Chinese manufacturer?

The industry standard is 30% deposit to start production and 70% balance before shipping. We accept wire transfers (T/T), Alibaba Trade Assurance, and PayPal for smaller amounts.

Can you help with sizing for different countries?

Yes. We have master size charts for the US, UK, EU, and AU markets. We can also grade your patterns into plus sizes up to 5XL.

What is the biggest mistake new brands make?

Rushing the sampling phase. It's better to spend an extra week perfecting the fit now than to have 500 pieces of unsellable stock later.
