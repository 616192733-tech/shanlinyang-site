Private Label Activewear Manufacturer Checklist for Leggings, Sports Bras, and Yoga Sets

Private-label activewear is more technical than many fashion categories. A beautiful sample is not enough. For leggings, sports bras, yoga sets, shorts, and athleisure collections, your manufacturer must understand fabric performance, fit pressure, opacity, stretch recovery, seam durability, branding, and packaging before production starts.

Quick answer

Choose a private label activewear manufacturer that can evaluate fabric opacity, four-way stretch, recovery, compression, waistband stability, sports bra support, seam strength, logo method, label placement, packaging, and size grading. If a supplier only asks for quantity and color, they are not asking enough questions for a serious activewear launch.

1. Fabric performance2. Fit and size grading3. Construction details4. Branding methods5. Sampling and testing6. US/EU shipping prep

1. Fabric performance comes before price

For activewear, fabric is not just a cost line. It determines comfort, confidence, review quality, and repeat purchase. Before choosing a supplier, ask about GSM, composition, hand feel, stretch direction, recovery, transparency, moisture feel, and whether the fabric is suitable for your intended activity level.

Yoga leggings usually need softness, four-way stretch, and comfort during movement. Gym leggings may need more compression and stronger recovery. Sports bras need support and stability. Tennis dresses and skirts may need structure plus mobility. A good supplier should not treat all “activewear fabric” as the same material.

2. Fit and size grading decide whether buyers reorder

Activewear fit is unforgiving. A small grading issue can create waist rolling, tight thighs, loose ankles, uncomfortable straps, or poor bust support. Ask whether your manufacturer can check measurement tolerance and advise on size range for your market.

For North America and Europe, do not assume one generic size chart will fit every customer. If you plan to sell XS–XXL or extend into plus size, discuss grading early. Plus-size activewear especially needs thoughtful waist, hip, thigh, rise, and bust adjustments rather than simple scaling.

3. Construction details protect performance

For activewear, seams, elastic, gussets, linings, and logo application affect performance. Ask about flatlock or overlock seams, crotch gusset options, waistband construction, pocket placement, lining choices, and the best logo method for stretch fabric.

Leggings

Review waistband height, pocket options, crotch seam comfort, squat-test opacity, and compression level.

Sports bras

Review strap style, underband, removable cups, lining, support level, and logo placement.

Sets

Review fabric lot matching, color consistency, top-bottom sizing, packaging, and SKU planning.

4. Branding must match stretch fabric

Private-label activewear often uses heat-transfer logos, silicone printing, reflective prints, embroidery, woven labels, printed care labels, hangtags, and branded poly bags. But not every method works well on every fabric or position. A logo placed on a high-stretch area may crack, peel, or distort if the method is wrong.

Before bulk production, confirm logo size, color, placement, wash durability, label comfort, care label position, and packaging instructions. For Shopify, Amazon, or wholesale delivery, also confirm barcode labels, carton marks, and SKU separation.

5. Sampling should answer specific questions

A useful activewear sample should not simply “look nice.” It should answer whether the garment performs as intended. Ask your supplier to confirm fabric hand feel, stretch, opacity, waistband stability, support level, measurement, logo placement, label comfort, and packaging before moving into bulk.

  • Can the leggings pass a squat and stretch review?
  • Does the waistband stay stable when moving?
  • Does the sports bra support the activity level you are selling?
  • Are seams comfortable against the skin?
  • Does the logo method survive stretch and washing?
  • Are labels and packaging aligned with your brand?

6. US/EU buyer preparation

For North American and European activewear buyers, production planning should include delivery route and retail readiness. Confirm packing method, size/color separation, barcode labels, carton marks, poly-bag requirements, and whether DDP or Amazon FBA preparation is needed.

If you plan a launch date, build in time for sampling, revisions, bulk production, QC, packing, and shipping. Rushed activewear production increases the chance of fit, color, packaging, and delivery problems.
