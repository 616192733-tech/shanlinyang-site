Women's Loungewear Manufacturer in China: Soft Sets, Robes & Cozy Basics

PUBLISHED 2025-06-29 · SHANLINYANG APPAREL FACTORY TEAM · ~8 MIN READ

The global loungewear market has seen explosive growth over the last few years, shifting from simple sleepwear to elevated "athflow" styles that blur the lines between home comfort and outdoor chic. For fashion brands and boutique owners, finding the right women's loungewear manufacturer in China is the most critical step in launching a successful collection. China remains the undisputed leader in apparel manufacturing, offering an unparalleled combination of fabric innovation, skilled labor, and supply chain efficiency. This guide will walk you through the nuances of sourcing loungewear, from fabric selection to factory vetting.

The Landscape of Loungewear Manufacturing in China

China's garment industry is organized into powerful regional clusters, each specializing in specific product categories. When searching for a loungewear manufacturer, you will likely encounter factories in two primary hubs: Fujian and Guangdong provinces. Xiamen, located in Fujian, is a premier destination for high-quality knitwear and activewear-adjacent loungewear. The factories here, including Shanlinyang, are known for their precision and ability to handle technical fabrics that require delicate handling, such as modal and bamboo blends.

In Guangdong province, cities like Shantou and Dongguan are famous for their massive lingerie and knitwear ecosystems. Shantou, in particular, has a long history of producing intricate robes, lace-trimmed pajamas, and traditional sleepwear. Sourcing from these clusters gives brands access to thousands of fabric suppliers, trim vendors, and specialized sewing technicians within a small geographical area. This proximity drastically reduces lead times and allows for more competitive pricing even at lower volumes.

Modern Chinese factories have evolved significantly. They are no longer just "order takers" but innovation partners. Many top manufacturers now offer design services (ODM) where they provide a library of in-house styles that brands can customize with their own logos and labels. This is a game-changer for new DTC brands that want to go to market quickly without the expense of hiring a full-time design team.

Customization Options & Premium Fabrics

The hallmark of great loungewear is the "hand-feel." Consumers prioritize softness and breathability above all else. A professional women's loungewear manufacturer in China should offer a wide array of premium fabrics to choose from. Common staples include:

  • Modal and Bamboo: These are the gold standards for modern loungewear. They are incredibly soft, moisture-wicking, and eco-friendly.
  • Organic Cotton: For brands focusing on sustainability, organic cotton provides a natural, breathable feel that customers love.
  • Ribbed Knits: Perfect for trendy two-piece sets that need a bit of texture and stretch.
  • Mulberry Silk: For the high-end luxury segment, nothing beats the sheen and skin-care benefits of real silk robes and slips.
  • Double-Faced Fleece: Ideal for winter-focused "cozy" collections that require warmth without excessive bulk.

Beyond fabric, customization extends to every detail of the garment. You can specify the weight (GSM) of the fabric to ensure the perfect drape, choose custom dyes to match your brand's unique color palette, and select specialized finishes like peach-skin brushing for extra softness. Branding options such as high-density screen printing, subtle embroidery, or custom-woven jacquard waistbands allow your brand to stand out in a crowded marketplace.

Understanding MOQ & Production Timelines

One of the biggest hurdles for small businesses is the Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ). In the past, Chinese factories often required 1,000 pieces per style, which was prohibitive for boutiques. However, the rise of small-batch manufacturing has changed the landscape. Today, a flexible women's loungewear manufacturer in China like Shanlinyang typically offers MOQs as low as 100 pieces per color per style.

Why does MOQ exist? It’s primarily driven by fabric sourcing. Textile mills usually have their own MOQs for dyeing fabric a custom color. By working with a manufacturer that maintains a large stock of popular fabrics in various colors, you can often bypass these high minimums. This "flexible manufacturing" approach allows you to test the market with multiple styles and colors without overcommitting your capital.

Lead times are another crucial factor. A typical production cycle involves: 1. Sampling (7–12 days): Developing the initial prototype to verify fit and fabric. 2. Bulk Production (25–35 days): Once the sample is approved, the factory proceeds to cutting, sewing, and finishing. 3. Shipping (7–30 days): Depending on whether you choose air freight (fast but expensive) or sea freight (economical but slower).

Quality Control & Testing for Loungewear

Quality control is what separates a successful brand from one plagued by returns and negative reviews. Loungewear presents unique QC challenges because the fabrics are often stretchy and delicate. A reliable manufacturer should implement a multi-stage inspection process:

Fabric Inspection: Checking the raw materials for pilling, color shading, and defects before a single cut is made. This is especially important for delicate fabrics like silk or high-stretch modal. During Production (DPRO): Checking the first few pieces off the line to ensure the sewing tension is correct. Incorrect tension on knit fabrics can lead to "wavy" seams or threads that snap when the garment is stretched. Final Inspection: A comprehensive check of every finished garment. This includes measuring against the size spec sheet, checking for loose threads, and ensuring all labels and hangtags are correctly placed.

Furthermore, testing is vital. A good manufacturer will help you arrange third-party lab tests (such as SGS or ITS) to verify colorfastness (ensuring the dark robes don't bleed onto white sheets), shrinkage rates, and the absence of harmful chemicals. Providing your customers with these assurances builds long-term brand trust.

How to Start Your Private Label Journey

Launching your loungewear line doesn't have to be overwhelming. The process follows a logical progression that ensures your vision is accurately translated into a physical product. First, start with a Tech Pack. This is the blueprint for your garment, containing sketches, measurements, fabric specs, and branding details. If you don't have a tech pack, many factories can work from a physical reference sample—you send them a garment you love, and they reverse-engineer it for you.

Next, move to the Sampling Phase. Never skip this step to save time. The sample is your only way to feel the fabric and see how it fits on a real body. It's common to go through one or two rounds of revisions before the fit is perfect. Once you are 100% satisfied, you sign off on a "PP Sample" (Pre-Production sample), which serves as the gold standard for the bulk run.

Finally, focus on Packaging and Logistics. Loungewear is often purchased as a gift or a self-care treat, so the unboxing experience matters. Discuss custom polybags, tissue paper, and mailers with your manufacturer. A full-service factory can often handle the entire logistics chain, delivering the goods directly to your warehouse or even to a 3PL (third-party logistics) provider like Amazon FBA.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the typical MOQ for custom loungewear in China?

For small-batch manufacturers like Shanlinyang, the MOQ for custom loungewear typically starts at 100 pieces per color per style. This allows for customization of fabric, fit, and branding while keeping initial investment manageable for boutique brands.

What are the best fabrics for luxury loungewear?

Premium loungewear often uses modal, bamboo, mulberry silk, or high-grade cotton blends. Ribbed knits and soft fleeces are also popular for cozy winter sets. We recommend choosing OEKO-TEX certified fabrics for superior comfort and skin-friendliness.

How long does the sampling process take for loungewear?

Sampling usually takes 7 to 12 days. This includes source-matching your desired fabric, creating the pattern, and sewing the first prototype for your approval of fit and finish.

Can you handle private label loungewear branding?

Yes, we offer full private label services including custom woven neck labels, heat-transfer logos, custom hangtags, and branded eco-friendly packaging.

Where are the major loungewear production hubs in China?

Major hubs include Xiamen in Fujian province (specializing in high-quality knits and woven sets), and various districts in Guangdong province like Shantou, known for its extensive lingerie and loungewear supply chain.
