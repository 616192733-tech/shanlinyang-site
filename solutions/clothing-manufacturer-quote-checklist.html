Factory quote checklist

What to send a clothing manufacturer to get a faster, more accurate quote.

Many buyers ask for a price before the product is clear enough to quote. This checklist helps online brands, boutiques, and startup labels prepare the details a factory needs: style, fabric, quantity, sizing, branding, destination, and deadline.

Send my quote request Use the sourcing brief template

For custom OEM/ODM

Send a tech pack, sketch, reference photo, or sample. Include fabric, construction, size range, label, and packaging expectations.

For Shopify or DTC brands

Include launch date, warehouse country, packaging plan, barcode/FBA needs, and how many colors or SKUs you want to start with.

For boutique testing

Tell the factory whether you want stock wholesale first, a low-risk test order, or a private-label reorder path after validation.

The checklist

Send these 8 details before asking for price

1. Product category

Activewear, dresses, swimwear, loungewear, plus-size apparel, two-piece sets, or mixed collection.

2. Reference style

Photos, product links, sketches, CADs, tech packs, or a sample garment. Clear references reduce guessing.

3. Fabric direction

Stretch, compression, cotton handfeel, rib, terry, satin, recycled material, lining, GSM, or “match this sample”.

4. Quantity plan

Pieces per style, color, and size. If unsure, say whether you are thinking 100, 300, 500, or 1,000+ pieces.

5. Size range

Standard XS–XL, extended XXL, plus XL–5XL, or your own brand size chart.

6. Branding

Logo print, embroidery, woven neck label, care label, hangtag, barcode, branded poly bag, or retail packaging.

7. Destination

United States, Canada, UK, Germany, France, EU warehouse, Amazon FBA, 3PL warehouse, or boutique address.

8. Deadline

Sample deadline, photo-shoot date, launch date, event date, or target delivery week.

Good vs weak enquiry

A better enquiry gets a better factory answer

Weak enquiryBetter enquiryWhy it matters
“How much for leggings?”“High-waist leggings, 75% nylon / 25% spandex feel, 300 pcs, black and sage, XS–XL, logo on waistband, ship to California.”The factory can estimate fabric, cutting, logo, size breakdown, and logistics.
“Can you make this dress?”“Midi dress from reference photo, lined bodice, satin feel, 100 pcs per color, S–XL, woven label and hangtag, launch in 10 weeks.”The factory can assess complexity, fabric sourcing, sample route, and schedule risk.
“Need cheap activewear.”“Startup activewear brand, first capsule of leggings and sports bras, target 100–300 pcs/style, need sample-first route and MOQ advice.”The factory can recommend a safer launch plan instead of guessing only by price.

If you do not have all details yet, use the sourcing brief template and send what you know.

Quote questions

Common questions before requesting a clothing factory quote

Can I get a quote without a tech pack?

Yes. A tech pack is helpful, but an early quote discussion can start from reference photos, product links, sketches, or a physical sample. The more complete the details, the more accurate the price.

Why do factories ask for quantity first?

Quantity changes fabric MOQ, cutting efficiency, trim ordering, labor planning, packaging, and shipping. A 100-piece test run and a 1,000-piece reorder are planned differently.

Should I ask for FOB, DDP, or landed cost?

If you are comparing factories, FOB pricing is useful. If you want to understand your final cost to the US or Europe, ask for DDP or destination logistics options as well.

What if I am not ready for custom production?

You can start with stock wholesale or a low-risk test route, then move winning styles into private-label or custom production after validation.
