Velvet Tracksuit Manufacturer: Wholesale Velour Sets from China

The velvet tracksuit (or more accurately, the velour tracksuit) is a timeless fashion staple that has made a massive comeback in recent years. From the iconic designs of the early 2000s to the modern, luxury loungewear aesthetic of today, velvet sets remain a top-selling category for boutique owners and online brands. However, producing a high-quality velvet tracksuit requires a specific type of expertise and the right fabric sourcing.

In this guide, we'll dive into the world of velvet manufacturing, explaining the different fabric types, branding techniques, and how to find a reliable partner in China to bring your velvet collection to life.

Velvet vs. Velour: Which One for Your Tracksuits?

While the terms are often used interchangeably, there is a technical difference. Velvet is traditionally a woven fabric with a dense pile. It's beautiful but has very little stretch, making it better for dresses or formal wear. Velour, on the other hand, is a knitted fabric. It has the same plush, soft pile as velvet but offers the stretch and recovery needed for activewear and loungewear.

Most "velvet tracksuits" on the market today are actually made from high-grade velour. At Shanlinyang, we source premium velour blends (typically 80% cotton and 20% polyester or 95% polyester and 5% spandex) that provide the perfect balance of luxury feel and everyday comfort. When speaking with your manufacturer, be sure to specify "stretch velour" if you want that classic tracksuit fit.

Customizing Your Velvet Collection: Colors and Cuts

One of the biggest advantages of working with a custom velvet tracksuit manufacturer is the ability to choose your own colors. While black and navy are always safe, velvet truly shines in deep, rich tones like emerald green, burgundy, or royal blue. These colors catch the light beautifully and give the garments a "high-end" look.

In terms of cuts, the "relaxed fit" hoodie and "wide-leg" pant combination is currently trending for a modern loungewear look. For a more nostalgic, "Y2K" aesthetic, a slim-fit zip-up hoodie and flared pants are the way to go. We can help you develop custom patterns for both, ensuring your brand offers a unique silhouette that fits your target audience perfectly.

Branding Velvet: Embroidery vs. Rhinestones

How you brand your velvet tracksuits can drastically change the vibe of the collection. Embroidery is the gold standard for luxury branding. It's durable and adds a physical texture to the garment. However, because velour has a thick pile, the embroidery needs to be executed with a high stitch count and a proper stabilizer to prevent the logo from "sinking" into the fabric.

For a more glamorous or retro look, Rhinestones (Crystals) are a popular choice. High-quality hot-fix rhinestones can be applied in intricate patterns or to spell out your brand name. Other options include subtle woven labels on the hem or custom metal hardware (zipper pulls and aglets) for a truly premium finish. At Shanlinyang, we have the specialized equipment needed to handle all of these branding techniques on plush fabrics.

Quality Control for Plush Fabrics

Manufacturing with velour presents unique challenges. The "nap" or direction of the pile must be consistent across all panels of the garment. If one sleeve is cut "up-nap" and the other "down-nap," they will appear to be different colors when the light hits them. This is a common mistake made by low-end factories that are trying to save fabric during the cutting process.

Another issue is "pile crushing" during shipping. If the tracksuits are packed too tightly, the pile can become flattened or creased. We use specific folding and packing techniques, often including individual poly bags with a bit of air, to ensure your velvet sets arrive at your doorstep looking fresh and luxurious. Our internal quality control team checks every single piece for nap consistency and seam strength before packing.

Sourcing Wholesale Velvet Sets from China

If you are a boutique owner looking for a faster turnaround, you might consider sourcing our "ready-to-ship" wholesale velvet sets. These are styles we have already designed and tested, which you can purchase in smaller quantities. This is a great way to test the market with different colors before committing to a full custom production run.

Even with wholesale items, you can still add your own branding. We offer "relabeling" services where we replace our factory labels with your custom brand labels. This allows you to launch your velvet line in weeks rather than months. Whether you choose custom OEM or private label wholesale, our focus is on providing the highest quality velour on the market.

Logistics and Lead Times for Velvet Production

Velvet production lead times are slightly longer than for basic cotton items, typically 35-45 days for a bulk run. This is because the fabric often needs to be custom-dyed and handled with extra care during the sewing stage. For sampling, you can expect a 10-15 day turnaround.

Because velvet is a bulkier fabric, shipping costs per item will be slightly higher than for t-shirts or thin activewear. We recommend calculating your margins carefully and considering sea freight for larger orders to keep your unit costs down. Our logistics team can provide you with detailed quotes for both air and sea shipping to help you make an informed decision.
