Spandex Clothing Manufacturer: Stretch Fabric Sourcing for Activewear

PUBLISHED 2025-06-25 · SHANLINYANG APPAREL FACTORY TEAM · ~8 MIN READ

The invention of spandex (also known as elastane or Lycra) transformed the clothing industry, enabling the creation of form-fitting, supportive, and flexible garments that were previously impossible. Today, spandex is a critical component of everything from high-performance activewear to comfortable lifestyle basics. For brand owners, partnering with a specialized spandex clothing manufacturer is essential to ensuring that your products have the right balance of stretch, recovery, and durability.

At Shanlinyang Apparel, we've mastered the technical challenges of working with high-stretch fabrics. We understand how different spandex blends behave during the knitting, cutting, and sewing processes, and we use this expertise to deliver superior products for our global clients. This guide explores the technical side of spandex manufacturing and how to source the best stretch fabrics for your brand.

Understanding Spandex Blends and Compositions

Spandex is rarely used on its own; it's almost always blended with other fibers like nylon, polyester, cotton, or rayon. The percentage of spandex determines the elasticity of the fabric. For activewear and compression garments, a blend of 10%–20% spandex is common, providing the "snap-back" power needed for high-intensity movement. For lifestyle basics, a lower percentage (3%–5%) provides just enough stretch for comfort while maintaining the natural feel of the base fiber.

Nylon/spandex blends are the gold standard for performance activewear, offering a smooth, cool-to-the-touch feel and excellent durability. Polyester/spandex blends are more affordable and take vibrant prints exceptionally well, making them ideal for fashion-forward gym wear. As a specialized spandex clothing manufacturer, we help you choose the right blend based on your design goals and price point.

Technical Sewing and Construction for Stretch

Sewing spandex requires specialized equipment and techniques. Traditional straight stitches will snap when a stretch fabric is pulled, leading to garment failure. We use advanced industrial machines that produce "stretch stitches," such as four-needle six-thread (flatlock) and various overlock configurations. These stitches move with the fabric, ensuring that the seams are as elastic as the material itself.

Thread choice is also critical. We use high-quality polyester or nylon threads that are specifically designed for high-stretch applications. Tension control is the final piece of the puzzle; our experienced technicians calibrate every machine to match the specific elasticity of the fabric being used. By focusing on these technical details, we ensure that your spandex clothing is built to last through countless workouts and washes.

Managing Stretch Recovery and Fabric Skewing

One of the biggest challenges in spandex manufacturing is ensuring "stretch recovery"—the ability of the fabric to return to its original shape after being stretched. Low-quality spandex can lose its elasticity over time, leading to "baggy" knees or waistbands. We source only high-grade spandex fibers that are known for their superior recovery properties, ensuring your garments maintain their fit for the life of the product.

We also implement strict fabric relaxation protocols. Spandex fabric is often under tension when it's wound onto rolls at the mill. If it's cut immediately, it will shrink significantly, leading to sizing errors. We allow all spandex fabrics to "relax" for 24–48 hours before cutting, ensuring that the dimensions are stable and accurate. This attention to detail is what separates a professional spandex clothing manufacturer from a standard garment factory.

Quality Control: The Squat Test and More

Quality control for spandex clothing focuses on performance and opacity. For leggings and activewear, the "squat test" is a non-negotiable part of our QC process. We ensure that the fabric remains opaque even when stretched to its limit. we also test for color fastness, ensuring that the vibrant colors of your collection don't bleed or fade when washed with other items.

We check every batch for "grinning"—the base white or transparent fibers showing through the surface when the fabric is stretched. We also monitor for pilling and "thread-out," where the internal elastic fibers break and show on the surface of the garment. By maintaining these rigorous standards, we help you build a brand that is synonymous with quality and performance in the competitive activewear market.

Private Labeling and Custom Branding

Branding spandex garments requires techniques that don't crack or peel when the fabric is stretched. Silicone heat transfers are our most popular option, offering a high-definition, flexible finish that moves perfectly with the material. We also offer woven elastics for waistbands, which provide a premium, branded look while enhancing the support and fit of the garment.

Custom packaging is the final touch for your private label collection. From branded poly bags to specialized hang tags that detail the technical features of your spandex fabric, we provide everything you need to create a professional brand presence. Whether you're a startup or an established label, Shanlinyang Apparel is your partner in technical fashion, providing the expertise and capacity you need to succeed.
