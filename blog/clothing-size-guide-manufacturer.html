Clothing Size Guide for Ordering from China: US, UK & EU Size Conversion

One of the most common challenges for fashion brand founders is getting the sizing right when manufacturing in China. A "Medium" in a Chinese factory often feels like an "Extra Small" in the United States or Europe. This discrepancy leads to high return rates and customer frustration. In this comprehensive clothing size guide manufacturer China focus, we break down how to bridge the gap between Asian and Western sizing for a successful product launch.

1. Understanding the Root of Sizing Discrepancies

When working with a clothing size guide manufacturer in China, the first thing to understand is that "Standard Sizing" doesn't truly exist globally. Sizing standards are based on the average body proportions of a local population. In China, the average height and build are smaller than in the US or Northern Europe. Consequently, "Standard Asian Sizing" is significantly smaller across the chest, shoulders, and hips.

If you simply tell a factory to produce "Size M," they will likely use their internal block pattern based on the local market. For a US brand, this is a recipe for disaster. To avoid this, you must explicitly define your target market (e.g., US Missy, UK Petite, or EU Standard) and provide a corresponding measurement chart in centimeters or inches. Never assume the factory's "M" is your "M."

2. US vs. UK vs. EU Size Conversion Charts

Navigating different international standards requires a clear conversion strategy. While there is some variation between brands, here is a general guideline for women's apparel that your clothing size guide manufacturer in China should follow:

  • US to UK: Generally, UK sizes are two numbers larger. A US 4 is a UK 8; a US 8 is a UK 12.
  • US to EU: US sizes are converted by adding 32 (generally). A US 4 is an EU 36; a US 10 is an EU 42.
  • UK to EU: A UK 10 is an EU 38; a UK 14 is an EU 42.

It is best practice to include a multi-market size chart on your website but only give your manufacturer the specific garment measurements (e.g., "Size M Chest: 96cm"). Providing the raw measurements removes the ambiguity of "Size Names."

3. The Importance of Tech Packs and Spec Sheets

The only way to ensure your clothing size guide manufacturer in China produces what you need is through a professional Tech Pack. A Tech Pack is the "blueprint" of your garment. It includes a Specification Sheet (Spec Sheet) that lists every measurement for every size you plan to produce (the "grading").

Your Spec Sheet should include:

  • Point of Measure (POM): Clear descriptions of where to measure (e.g., 1 inch below armhole).
  • Base Size: Usually your Medium, which the pattern is built from.
  • Grading Rules: How much the garment grows or shrinks between sizes (e.g., 2cm difference in chest per size).
  • Tolerances: The acceptable margin of error (e.g., +/- 1cm).

4. How to Conduct Proper Fit Sessions

Before moving to bulk production, you must verify the sizing through samples. A clothing size guide manufacturer in China will send you a "Prototype" or "Fit Sample." You should test this on a live fit model that represents your target customer's body type. Measuring the garment on a flat table is not enough; you need to see how the fabric drapes and moves on a human body.

Check for:

  • Proportion: Is the waist hitting at the right spot?
  • Ease: Is there enough room for movement without the garment looking baggy?
  • Length: Is the sleeve or hem length appropriate for your target height?

Send photos and clear "Fit Comments" back to the factory. If the Medium fits perfectly, ask for a "Size Set" to ensure the Small and Large are also graded correctly.

5. Communicating Tolerances and Quality Control

In mass garment manufacturing, no two items are exactly identical. A reputable clothing size guide manufacturer in China will work within "Tolerances." If your spec says the waist should be 70cm and the tolerance is +/- 1cm, any garment between 69cm and 71cm is considered passing.

However, if your factory consistently hits the lower end of the tolerance, your clothes will feel "small." During your final quality control inspection, make sure the inspector measures a representative percentage of the bulk order against your Spec Sheet. If the sizing is off, it's much easier to fix at the factory than after the goods have arrived in your warehouse.
