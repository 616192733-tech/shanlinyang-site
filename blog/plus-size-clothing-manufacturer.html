Plus Size Clothing Manufacturer in China: XL to 5XL Production Guide

PUBLISHED 2026-06-22 · SHANLINYANG APPAREL FACTORY TEAM · ~10 MIN READ

The "Body Positivity" movement is not just a trend; it's a fundamental shift in the global fashion industry. Despite this, many brands still struggle to find a **plus size clothing manufacturer** that truly understands the nuances of curvy fits. In 2025, providing inclusive sizing is no longer optional—it's a competitive necessity for any brand targeting the US, UK, or Australian markets.

At Shanlinyang Apparel, we've specialized in the production of plus-size women's clothing for over a decade. In this guide, we'll explain why manufacturing for XL to 5XL requires a different approach than standard sizing and how to choose the right partner in China.

1. The Art of Plus-Size Pattern Grading

The most common mistake brands make when searching for a **plus size clothing manufacturer in China** is assuming that an XL is simply a "scaled-up" version of a Small. This leads to garments that are too long in the arms, tight in the bust, or ill-fitting in the rise.

Professional plus-size manufacturing requires anatomical grading. This means:

  • Adjusting the bust-to-waist-to-hip ratios specifically for larger bodies.
  • Adding extra room in the upper arms and thighs.
  • Ensuring necklines and shoulder widths are proportionate.
  • Using reinforced stitching in high-stress areas like the inner thighs.

2. Fabric Selection: Comfort Meets Structure

For plus-size apparel, the fabric has to work harder. It needs to provide enough stretch for comfort while maintaining enough structure to offer a flattering silhouette. At Shanlinyang, we recommend:

  • High-GSM Knits: Heavier weights (250-320 GSM) ensure the fabric is opaque and doesn't "thin out" when stretched.
  • Spandex Blends: A minimum of 5-8% Spandex/Lycra is vital for shape retention.
  • Breathable Naturals: Cotton and Rayon blends are preferred for everyday wear to prevent heat buildup.

3. Navigating International Size Charts

Sizing is the number one cause of returns in the plus-size market. When working with a Chinese manufacturer, you must be explicit about your target market's standards. A "Chinese 5XL" is often equivalent to a "US Large."

FAQ: Plus Size Manufacturing

Do you charge more for plus-size garments?

Because plus-size garments use significantly more fabric (sometimes 50-80% more than a size Small) and require specialized patterns, the unit price is typically 10-20% higher than standard sizing.

Can you help me design a plus-size size chart?

Yes. We have historical data for US, UK, and Australian plus-size markets and can provide a 'starting' size chart that you can refine for your specific brand.

What is the best way to fit-test plus-size samples?

We always recommend using a real person (fit model) in your target size. If that's not possible, we use specialized plus-size mannequins that replicate realistic body proportions.

Are your fabrics 'squat-proof'?

Yes, especially for our plus-size activewear. We use high-density knits that remain opaque even when stretched over curvy hips.

Can I order just one size (e.g., only 3XL)?

Yes, if you have a specific niche. However, most brands find better success offering a range (e.g., XL to 3XL or 2XL to 5XL).
